## Experience

### Principal Designer & Researcher — Avalanche Canada (2023–present)
*Remote / Revelstoke, Canada*

Lead designer and researcher for digital products serving ~140,000+ public
users and 60+ professional forecasters across Avalanche Canada, the Colorado
Avalanche Information Center (CAIC), and Parks Canada.

- Shipped **AvIDdx**, the primary analysis tool used by avalanche forecasters in
  Canada and Colorado. Brings together weather, field observations, snowpack
  models, and satellite data into a single interactive workspace — replacing
  workflows that previously relied on experience and intuition alone.
- Shipped **AvObs**, a tool for analyzing avalanche observation reports from the
  field. Originated as PhD research and now runs in production — used daily by
  forecasters to assess where and how the snowpack is failing.
- Designed and built **WxObs**, a weather monitoring tool that pulls from hundreds
  of sources — weather stations, snow pillows, ski resorts, snow study plots —
  and computes summaries forecasters actually need. Includes a quality control
  system where senior staff flag or correct bad data in real time, something
  most weather tools don't support at this time scale.
- Redesigned key parts of the Avalanche Canada public website: a clearer danger
  rating legend, colorblind-safe colors, and a new basemap built around how
  backcountry users think about terrain — showing peaks, trailheads, and
  landmarks, with satellite imagery at close zoom for route-finding.
- Built a **place search system** with a ranking algorithm that learns from usage
  patterns and weighs location type, popularity, and proximity to return the
  most useful results first.
- Created a **user research panel** — an opt-in registry of backcountry users
  profiled by experience level, activity type, and region, linked to website
  analytics. Gives us a standing pool of participants for studies and a way to
  connect what people say with what they actually do on the site.
- Designed the **CAIC highway forecast email** — a daily briefing delivered to
  snow plow crews in Colorado and Alaska. Deliberately low-tech and robust for
  field conditions.
- Initiated a partnership with **Statistics Canada** to add a question about
  backcountry winter recreation to a national survey (running now, results
  expected early 2027). For the first time, Canada will have a reliable estimate
  of how many people are exposed to avalanche risk — data the organization
  needs to secure federal funding.
- Leading design strategy for the next generation of avalanche forecasting
  tools — moving from hand-drawn regional forecasts toward model-driven,
  location-specific hazard information. Designing a new app with CAIC (beta
  March 2026) — the first product in the avalanche industry to launch through
  a structured beta program.
- Mentoring a junior designer and coaching management on design practice.
  Embedded in the dev team — write code, review pull requests, and build
  working prototypes that get handed off to production. Partner with the
  research officer on snowpack modeling and user research strategy.
- Driving a culture of user-centered thinking across the organization through
  reading groups, workshops, and cross-team collaboration. Produce graphics and
  data analyses for government relations, public communications, and social
  media.
