Principal Designer & Researcher — Avalanche Canada
2023–present
Remote / Revelstoke, Canada

Lead designer and researcher at Avalanche Canada, responsible for all digital products used by 140,000+ public users and 60+ professional forecasters across Avalanche Canada, the Colorado Avalanche Information Center (CAIC), and Parks Canada.

  • Shipped AvIDdx, the primary forecaster analysis tool in Canada and Colorado. Integrates weather, field observations, snowpack models, and satellite data into one workspace — moving forecasting from purely experiential methods toward quantitative, human-machine approaches.
  • Shipped AvObs, a tool for analyzing observed avalanches to characterize hazard conditions. Evolved from PhD research into a production system used daily by forecasters.
  • Designed and built WxObs, a weather monitoring tool aggregating hundreds of sources (weather stations, snow pillows, ski resorts, snow study plots). Includes a metadata management system and QA/QC pipeline for sub-daily timescales — uncommon in operational meteorology.
  • Redesigned public-facing elements of avalanche.ca: danger rating legend, colorblind-safe palette, and a custom basemap showing peaks, trailheads, and landmarks with satellite imagery at close zoom.
  • Built a place search system with ranking based on usage patterns, location type, popularity, and proximity.
  • Created a user research panel profiling backcountry users by experience, activity, and region, linked to site analytics (Mixpanel). Standing participant pool for studies.
  • Designed the CAIC highway forecast email — daily briefing for snow plow crews in Colorado and Alaska. Low-tech, robust.
  • Initiated a Statistics Canada partnership to add backcountry recreation to a national survey (results early 2027) — first reliable estimate of avalanche-exposed population in Canada.
  • Leading design strategy for next-generation forecasting: model-driven, location-specific hazard products replacing hand-drawn regional forecasts. New app with CAIC launching beta March 2026.
  • Mentoring a junior designer and coaching management on design practice. Embedded in dev team: write code, review PRs, build prototypes for production handoff. Partner with research officer on modeling strategy and user research. Driving user-centered thinking across the organization through reading groups, workshops, and cross-team collaboration.
